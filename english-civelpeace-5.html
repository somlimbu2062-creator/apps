Civil Peace

Summary

"Civil Peace" is a short story by Nigerian author Chinua Achebe. The story takes place in a post-war Nigeria, following the end of the Biafran conflict. The main character, Jonathan Iwegbu, is a survivor of the war and is now trying to rebuild his life.

Jonathan is a happy man, despite the fact that he has lost all of his possessions during the war. He is excited about the opportunities that the new Nigeria has to offer, and is optimistic about the future. He has found a job, and is earning enough money to provide for his family. Despite this, however, he is constantly faced with obstacles and challenges.

One day, Jonathan's bicycle is stolen, and he is forced to walk to work. Along the way, he is stopped by a soldier who demands a bribe. Jonathan refuses to pay the bribe and is beaten as a result. Despite this, he remains hopeful and optimistic.

A few days later, Jonathan is approached by a former schoolmate, who is now a successful businessman. The businessman offers Jonathan a job, and promises to help him rebuild his life. Jonathan is overjoyed at the opportunity, and accepts the job.

However, Jonathan soon discovers that the businessman is involved in illegal activities, and he is forced to make a difficult decision. He decides to leave the job, and to return to his previous life. Despite this setback, Jonathan remains positive and optimistic, and is determined to rebuild his life again.

The story concludes with Jonathan reflecting on his experiences, and realizing that happiness comes from within. He realizes that he does not need material possessions or wealth to be happy, and that he is grateful for the peace and stability that he now enjoys.

"Civil Peace" is a powerful and thought-provoking story that highlights the resilience and determination of the human spirit. It is a story about hope, perseverance, and the importance of inner peace.

Jonathan thinks of himself as 'extraordinarily lucky' because he has managed to survive the Nigerian Civil War with his family intact and his house still standing. This positive outlook and gratitude help him to see the good in his life, even in the midst of difficult circumstances.

Jonathan mistrusts the officer who wants to take his bicycle because the situation in Nigeria was chaotic and uncertain in the aftermath of the civil war. The country was in disarray and there was a lack of security, with many people taking advantage of the chaos to enrich themselves. Jonathan is afraid that the officer might be one of these opportunists who are trying to take advantage of the situation, and so he mistrusts him and is wary of giving him his bicycle. This mistrust of the authorities and the general uncertainty in the country highlights the instability and lack of security in Nigeria during this time.

Jonathan's expression "Nothing puzzles God" reflects his strong faith in God, which he relies on to help him through difficult times. He believes that God is capable of solving even the most complex of problems, and this expression reveals his character as a person who is religious, optimistic, and resilient. At the beginning of the story, Jonathan is depicted as a content and grateful person, who is thankful for his five blessings and is optimistic about his future. Despite these difficulties, he remains determined to overcome them and maintain his positive outlook. Throughout the story, Jonathan's character is shown to be strong, resilient, and resourceful, and his experiences serve to strengthen his faith in God and his resolve to overcome adversity.

f. The title of the story "Civil Peace" itself is ironical as there is little to differentiate ‘civil peace’ from ‘civil war’. Do you think that the title of this story is appropriate, or would “Civil War” have been a better title? Explain.

The title "Civil Peace" is appropriate as it reflects the irony in the situation. The concept of civil peace in the aftermath of a civil war is meant to bring stability, security, and peace to the citizens, but the reality is often different. In the story, Jonathan experiences many challenges despite the supposed civil peace, including having his bicycle taken away by an officer and being visited by thieves. The use of the word "peace" highlights the contrast between what is promised and what is actually experienced by the characters, making it an effective and appropriate title.

However, an argument could be made for "Civil War" as an alternative title, as the events in the story highlight the ongoing struggles and challenges that exist even after the official end of a conflict. The choice of title ultimately depends on the perspective of the reader and the purpose of the story.
